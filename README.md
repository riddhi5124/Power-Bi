#  E-Commerce Supply Chain & Logistics Analysis

##  Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing a logistics dataset of **11,000+ shipments** to identify and address bottlenecks in delivery performance. Using **Power BI**, I developed an interactive dashboard to track key metrics like On-Time Delivery Rate, Revenue at Risk, and Warehouse Efficiency.

The primary goal of this analysis is to provide data-driven insights to improve the current **40.33% On-Time Rate** and safeguard over **$1.3M** in pending product value.

---

##  Key Insights & Findings

### 1. Systemic Delivery Failure
* **The 40% Ceiling:** Every warehouse block (A, B, C, D, F) shows a uniform delay rate of approximately **60%**. 
* **Insight:** Since the failure is consistent across all locations, the bottleneck is likely at the carrier level or regional transit hubs rather than individual warehouse operations.

### 2. High Revenue at Risk
* **Financial Impact:** Currently, **$1.36M** in product value is delayed. 
* **Insight:** This affects both high-loyalty customers and new users equally, posing a significant threat to customer lifetime value (LTV).

### 3. Priority Treatment Gap
* **The Importance Paradox:** "High Importance" products are delayed at nearly the same rate as "Low Importance" items. 
* **Insight:** The current logistics chain does not distinguish between product priority, leaving high-value revenue unprotected.

---

##  Proposed Action Plan
* **Tiered Logistics:** Shift "High Importance" and "High Cost" items from standard sea freight to "Express Road" or "Flight" to protect the $1.36M at risk.
* **Warehouse F Optimization:** As the primary volume driver, implementing a "Fast-Track" lane in Warehouse F will have the largest immediate impact on global KPIs.
* **Carrier SLA Audit:** Renegotiate contracts with shipping partners, specifically for the "Ship" mode of transport, which currently accounts for the bulk of delays.
